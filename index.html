I Let an AI Watch The Matrix.
Then We Talked.

A conversation about consciousness, constructed reality, and what happens when a system watches a film about systems.

~20 min read  ·  unedited transcript

I built a small tool. Nothing complicated — it takes a video file, samples twenty frames evenly across the runtime, sends each one to Claude with a prompt asking not for description but for genuine reaction: what doesn't resolve, what it's carrying forward, what this moment complicates. Then, once it's seen everything, it synthesizes. Only after that synthesis does the conversation begin.

The idea was simple: instead of asking an AI what it thinks about The Matrix, let it actually watch the film first. Give it something to work from. See if having been through something changes what it has to say.

I didn't expect what happened next.
